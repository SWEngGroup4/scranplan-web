Scranplan Development Team

James Pearson

Project Leader

Responsible for organising the development, design and marketing teams by setting objectives, tracking workload and re-directing resources if neccesary.

James Clawley

Lead Programmer

Whilst all members of the team contribute to the Code within the project the Lead Programmer has the final say when it comes to how our code is layed out and how it all fits together. They also conduct a code review along with the T&I manager to make sure the app we deliver is up to scratch.

Jun Ma

XML/Server Manager

Responsible for maintaining the structuring the database Scranplan uses.
Also involved in making sure costs for database storage don't spiral and our application is able to accept the appropriate universal XML format we need for displaying your beautiful recipes.

Becky Anderson

Marketing Manager

Every company needs to sell there product and in many ways one of the most essential roles is the marketing manager. They represent the companies vision  whilst sticking to the customer specification to produce a set of campaigns, either in person or online that help the rest of our team take in the essential advice you provide.

Louis Newman

Finance Manager

No company is successful without active financial management, in most cases the financial side becomes the key assessor of the success of a company. The finance manager is to take lead on all company finances ensuring Scranplan runs smoothly, makes a profit and doesn’t go bankrupt.

Nathan Billis

Testing and Integration Manager

The T&I manager is in charge of the processes behind testing the code and integrating it together with other modules in the project.
Like the lead programmer they understand the project as a whole and conduct code review and testing reports to make sure members of the team don't accidently destroy the app.

Joe Butler

UI & Graphic Designer

In order to provide a good user interaction the UI & Graphic designer develops a link between the feedback given by the users during testing and the appropriate actions to be taken by the project team in order to make the UI elements within the project both appropriate and appealing to the correct target audience.
